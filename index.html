Anxiety

Are you experiencing persistent worry, racing thoughts, irrational fears, sleep disturbance, increased heart rate, irritability, unusual sweating, fatigue or difficulty concentrating? You may be suffering from Generalized Anxiety Disorder(GAD).

Depression

Are you experiencing intense sadness, isolation, loss of interest, increased or decreased appetite, unexpected weight changes, low energy, pessimistic thinking, hopelessness, restlessness or agitation? Major Depression Disorder could be the cause.

PTSD

Are you experiencing recurrent, unwanted and distressing thoughts of a traumatic life event? Do you have severe emotional distress, physical reactions, upsetting dreams, nightmares or flashbacks of the experience?

Bipolar

Are you having manic episodes that include symptoms such as high energy, reduced sleep, and loss touch with reality? Mood episodes can last from days to months at a time. We can help you with this through both medication management and therapy.

Panic Disorder

Are you have recurring or regular panic attacks, often and for no apparant reason? This may include nausea, sweating, trembling, and a sensation that your heart is beating irregularly. Our aim is to reduce the number of panic attacks and easte the severity of your symptoms.

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder

Are you having patterns of unwanted thoughts and fears that lead to you to repetitive behaviors? These can interfere with daily activities and cause increased distress. We offer treatment for OCD, which include cognitive behavioral therapy(CBT)with a licensed therapist.

Schizophrenia

Are you having hallucinations, delusions, and extremely unorderly thinking? Schozophrenia is a disorder that can be extremely disruptive with daily functioning.

Attention Deficit/
Hyperactivity Disorder

Are you having difficulty concentrating or focusing? Having hyperactivity and impulsiveness? Attention Deficit/Hperactivity Disorder(ADHD) can effect your focus, organizational skills, and forgetfullness. At Helios we have an array of testing to diagnose and treat ADHD.
